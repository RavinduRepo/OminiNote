Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 8, 2026

OminiNote ("the app") is a stylus note-taking application developed by Ravindu. This policy explains how the app handles your data.

What data we access

The app optionally lets you sign in with your Google account to sync your notes to Google Drive. When you sign in, the app:

  • Reads your Google account email address (displayed in the sync settings UI).
  • Creates, reads, and updates files inside the omininote/ folder in your own Google Drive using the drive.file scope. This scope is limited to files the app itself created — the app cannot see any other files in your Drive.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not collect, transmit, or store your data on any server operated by us.
  • We do not share your data with any third party.
  • We do not use analytics, advertising SDKs, or crash-reporting services.
  • We do not access any Drive files outside the omininote/ folder.

Local storage

Notes, pages, and assets are stored locally on your device in the app's documents directory. They are never uploaded anywhere except your own Google Drive when you choose to enable sync.

Google Drive sync (optional)

Sync is entirely optional. You may use the app indefinitely without signing in to Google. If you enable sync, all data travels directly between your device and your personal Google Drive account over Google's encrypted infrastructure — we have no visibility into it.

Revoking access

You can sign out and revoke the app's Drive access at any time from the app's Settings screen, or from Google Account → Security → Third-party apps. Revoking access does not delete notes already saved to Drive; you may delete the omininote/ folder from Drive manually at any time.

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new effective date.
